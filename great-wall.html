About the Great Wall

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials. Built to protect the Chinese states and empires from invasion, it stretches over 13,000 miles.

Key Facts:

Feature Details
Country China
Year of Construction 7th Century BC to 16th Century AD
Length 21,196 km (13,171 miles)
Significance Longest man-made structure in the world
